Cocchi Barolo Chinato

Renowned in Piemonte since its introduction in 1891, Cocchi Barolo Chinato is a wine of D.O.C.G. Barolo infused with quinine bark, rhubarb, ginger, cardamom, cocoa, and a variety of other aromatic spices. Round and rich, yet with the deep back palate once expects of Barolo, this wine possesses the definition necessary to be an ideal digestif or pairing with dark chocolate. Serve as you would serve high-quality Port or Madeira, or use as a luxurious alternative to sweet red vermouth. You would not be alone in finding this to be the Rolls Royce (or Maserati) of Italian dessert wines.

The DOCG Barolo, for nebbiolo-based wines from a specific zone in Piemonte, is possibly the most famous appellation in all of Italy. The wines are profoundly perfumed, with noses of rose, tar, iodine, and red fruit, and the best vintages are extremely long-lived, the tannins and acids unfolding to produce deeper fruit notes, cedar and redwood tones, and thrilling length. Virtually every producer of Barolo makes at least a little Barolo Chinato, which is Barolo wine sweetened, fortified and infused with quinine and spices. Barolo Chinato is the aristocratic digestif of Piemonte, and is a traditional way to use up Barolo that fails to make the cut for an estate’s premier bottlings. Cocchi’s Barolo holdings are in Scarrone, which is known just as much for Barbera as Barolo. In some years, overripeness is an issue. However, the bolder style of Barolo that this terroir yields is perfect for Barolo Chinato, as it provides a rich backbone for spices. Cocchi’s style of Barolo Chinato respects the terroir, using numerous sweet spices and a limited amount of additional bitterness to produce an opulent, friendly Barolo Chinato. Many other producers load their Chinato with wormwood, which clashes with the tannins of the wine to produced a clipped, herbaceous Chinato. The most traditional way to consume Cocchi Barolo Chinato is by itself, as a dessert wine. Its bitterness scrubs the palate of the fats and proteins of dark chocolate in a way no un-aromatized wine can. During wintertime, the fine bars of Asti will warm Barolo Chinato and serve it in a mug, simply garnished. In cocktails, the richness of Cocchi Barolo Chinato works wonders with profound whiskeys. In other substitutions for sweet red vermouth, Cocchi Barolo Chinato adds depth of flavor, rich spice and more than a hint of majesty.

About Giulio Cocchi Spumanti Srl

Giulio Cocchi has produced wine-based aperitifs and traditional Piemontese sparkling wines since 1891. Since 1978, Cocchi has been owned and operated by the Bava Family, themselves renowned wine producers in Monferrato and Langhe.

About Vini Amari

Outside of the “big three” of wormwood, gentian, and quinine, herbalists worldwide have used many avors as the backbones of aromatized wines. Many of these wines no longer exist, and many of those avorings have moved into spirit-based amari and liqueurs. A few examples of “vino amaro,” or wine-based amaro, still exist, and use herbal flavors that are harmonious and familiar to traditional drinkers worldwide. Because of their wine base, these products have vinous texture and a depth of avor that add a welcome breadth when used in mixed drinks.

Recipes

Chin-Chin

Build in a double rocks glass filled with ice:
1.5 oz Aperitivo Cappelletti
0.75 oz Cocchi Barolo Chinato
1.5 oz tonic water
Garnish with an orange peel.

Hugo Black

Stir with ice:
1.5 oz High West Rendezvous Rye
1.5 oz Cocchi Barolo Chinato
Strain into a coupe.
Garnish with an orange peel.

Darkside

Stir with ice:
2 oz Hayman’s London Dry Gin
1 oz Cocchi Barolo Chinato
2 dashes Peychaud’s bitters
Strain into a coupe.
Garnish with a lime peel and a star anise pod.

Fort Point

Stir with ice:
2 oz rye whiskey
0.75 oz Cocchi Barolo Chinato
0.5 oz Rothman & Winter Orchard Apricot Liqueur
Strain into a coupe.
Garnish with lemon peel.

Noce Vecchio

Stir with ice:
1.5 oz Cocchi Barolo Chinato
0.75 oz Cocchi Americano Bianco
0.75 oz Dolin Dry Vermouth de Chambéry
Strain into a double rocks glass filled with ice.
Garnish with cheese and salami on a skewer or toothpick.

Booker’s Manhattan

Stir with ice:
1.5 oz Booker’s Barrel Proof bourbon
1.5 oz Cocchi Barolo Chinato
2 dashes Angostura Bitters
Strain into a coupe.
Garnish with an orange peel and a cherry.

Americano-Italiano

Stir with ice:
1 oz Cocchi Barolo Chinato
1 oz Aperitivo Cappelletti
1 oz Solbeso cacao spirit
Strain into a coupe.
Garnish with a grapefruit peel.
Serve with bittersweet chocolate.

Fair Thee Well

Stir with ice:
1.5 oz bourbon
0.75 oz Cocchi Barolo Chinato
0.75 oz Pierre Ferrand Dry Curaçao
Strain into a coupe.
Garnish with an orange peel.

Fair Isle

Shake with ice:
1 oz Cocchi Barolo Chinato
0.75 oz Scotch whisky
1 oz grapefruit juice
1 dash grapefruit bitters
Strain into a double rocks glass filled with ice.
Garnish with grapefruit peel.
