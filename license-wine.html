    Aperitivo Cappelletti

    Wines ➜ Aperitif/Aromatized ➜ Vini Amari

    Known throughout Italy, the category of “bitter” (sometimes called “red bitter”) includes spirit-based, wine-based, and non-alcoholic examples. As with other categories of amari, the oldest tend to be wine-based (before World War I, using large quantities of neutral spirit to produce a liqueur was cost-prohibitive). The defining characteristics of “bitter” include red color (classically natural, although most producers use an artificial dye now), a lot of gentian, and a lot of citrus. The Aperitivo Cappelletti, endearingly known to locals as just “Specialino,” may be the oldest style of the classic red bitter still in production. Unlike its larger commercial rivals, Cappelletti is less sweet and has a wonderful drying finish. Additionally, it still uses natural carmine color for a crimson red and retains a vinous texture and refreshing acidity from its wine base. Full details
